About Me

    My name is Tsun-Yu Yang. I obtained his Ph.D. degree, under the supervision of Prof. Ming-Chang Yang, from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK), Shatin, Hong Kong, in December 2023. From January 2024 to date, he has been a postdoctoral researcher at the Institute of Information Science (IIS) of Academia Sinica, working under the supervision of Dr. Yuan-Hao Chang. My research interests focus on various types of system designs, which include graph processing, emerging memory/storage technologies (e.g., Skyrmion racetrack memory, IMR-based HDD, Zoned Namespace SSD (ZNS), non-volatile memory), and graph neural networks. The objective of my research papers often revolves around three themes: cost-effectiveness, efficiency, and scalability. During my Ph.D. studies, I published eight papers in various conferences and journals, including 1 * USENIX ODSI, 2 * USENIX FAST, 1 * USENIX ATC, 1 * ACM ASPLOS, 1 * IEEE/ACM DAC, 1 * IEEE HPCA, and 1 * IEEE TCAD. Dr. Yang has garnered recognition for his academic contributions. Notably, he is awarded the Academia Sinica Postdoctoral Scholar, which is one of the highest honors for all postdoctoral researchers working in Academia Sinica. He also received the Hong Kong Ph.D. Fellowship (HKPFS) Award, the most prestigious honor for Ph.D. students studying in Hong Kong. Additionally, he received the CUHK Vice-Chancellor HKPFS scholarship, the 57th Design Automation Conference (DAC) Young Fellow, and the Best Teaching Assistant Award from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at CUHK. For academic services, I am currently serving as a reviewer for the IEEE Computer Architecture Letters (CAL) journal, and will serve as the Secretary of Technical Program Co-Chair for the ACM/IEEE Design Automation Conference (DAC) 2025.
      I am currently seeking a position as an assistant professor worldwide and I very much welcome all relevant opportunities. Please feel free to contact me for any open positions.
